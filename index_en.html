Relevant projects

Vertical scale for profiles of water vapor and sulfur compounds in the lower troposphere.

The Python 3 programming language was used in the Project to process the data from the CAMS Global Reanalysis monthly average fields (EAC4).

Vertical scales are calculated for profiles of water vapor and sulfur compounds in the lower troposphere in the period 2003 - 2020 and include multiple variables (relative humidity, sulfur dioxide mass mixing ratio, sulfate aerosol mixing ratio , Mixing ratio of SO2 precursors). With the help of the linear regression method, the relationship between the concentration of SO2 pollutants and the vertical coordinate (pressure in isobaric coordinates) is analyzed.
Python libraries used: -numpy -xarray -matplotlib -cartopy

Seedability determination in Cuba using one-dimensional cloud model.

In the present work, as input to a one-dimensional stationary cumulus cloud model, data from the NCEP/NCAR reanalysis from 2005 to 2009 were used to simulate aerological soundings in a grid located over Cuba (19.5 to 23.5º and -85 to -74º), with a resolution of 9 points in latitude and 12 points in longitude.

For this, the daily values of the 18Z of the geopotential height, the temperature and the wind (zonal and meridional) of the Mandatory levels from 1,000 hPa to 10 hPa, and relative humidity from 1,000 hPa to 300 hPa. With the use of the one-dimensional stationary model of cumulus cloud, the tops are calculated Seeded and unseeded cloud maximums with radii of 0.5 km, 1.0 km, 1.5 km, 2.0 km, and 2.5 km. Based on this information, the seedability index is determined and its spatiotemporal distribution over the national territory is obtained. This distribution indicates the areas with the greatest potential for cloud seeding work, which are preferably located in the western region from June to August, although there are also high seeding levels in the rest of the country during these months. The knowledge of the sowing potential and its spatio-temporal distribution allows the effective use of the laboratory plane and the necessary resources with a view to carrying out the campaigns and (or) the experiments to increase rainfall.

Relación de la longitud de Monin - Obukhov con el desarrollo de la convección en Camagüey.

In the research, they used measurements in the wind tower located in Jayama Camagüey (21º 21'N, 77º 52'W), are employed to determine the relationship between Monin Obukhov scale length (L) and convective processes that have place in the region.

The temperature, relative humidity, wind speed and wind direction data, taken at 10 minute intervals, at 10 and 100 m in height, plus the pressure at 10 m were used. After certain L were selected 73 days with existence of radar observations that met the condition that all or most L values calculated for the day period, of this days, whether or greater than zero or less, but not fluctuate around this value. These observations were used to determine the occurrence or absence of convection and the analysis thereof is determined depending on the values of L can be divided into 3 groups, cases with mostly cloudy days (positive L), strong convection days, with values very close to zero and L ranging around -53.85 m and weak convection days with L farthest zero and averaged -110.7 m. The analysis of samples confirmed that the three groups belong to different populations to a significance level of 95%.
